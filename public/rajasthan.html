Tradition

Tradition redefined, fashion reimagined.

Earlier: Rajasthani men wore Angrakha, Dhoti, and colorful Safa (turbans), while women wore Ghagra-Choli with heavy Odhni. Clothes were designed for desert weather and used vibrant colors to contrast the arid land.


Now: Modern wear is common in cities, but traditional attire is still worn proudly during festivals, weddings, and cultural events.


In short: Attire has transformed from daily wear to ceremonial elegance, while keeping Rajasthan’s royal identity alive.

Ornaments

Elegance Wrapped
in Heritage

Rajasthani jewellery is regal and elaborate. Women wear Borla (spherical maang tikka), Rajputi Aad (choker necklace), Bajuband (armlet), Kaanbali (earrings), and Payal (anklets). Men adorned Kada and gemstone rings.

Even today, brides wear traditional jewellery as a symbol of heritage, while modern adaptations are seen in fashion shows and luxury collections.

Art

Crafting Culture, Creating Beauty

Rajasthan’s art includes Pichwai paintings, Miniature paintings, Blue Pottery of Jaipur, Block printing of Sanganer, and Marble carving. These arts flourished under royal patronage and continue to define Rajasthani identity.

Today, these crafts are exported globally and displayed in fashion, interiors, and heritage exhibitions.

Food

Taste the colors of India

Rajasthani cuisine is rich, spicy, and designed for desert life. Famous dishes include Dal Baati Churma, Ker Sangri, Gatte ki Sabzi, Laal Maas, and Mawa Kachori. Food uses lots of ghee and dried ingredients to last in arid conditions.

The royal thali of Rajasthan is famous worldwide for its variety and flavors.

Folk Dance

Embrace the Rhythm of Indian Folk

Rajasthan is known for its mesmerizing folk dances. Ghoomar, performed by women in swirling ghagras, symbolizes grace and celebration. Kalbelia, performed by the snake charmer community, is vibrant and fast-paced. Chari dance uses pots with fire lamps, while Kachhi Ghodi depicts tales of local bandits.

These dances are performed in fairs, festivals, and weddings, representing the spirit of Rajasthan.
